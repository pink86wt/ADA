Frequently asked questions

The academic year at the Apple Developer Academy will start in September with no exceptions. Although we are doing our best to start in-person, the situation with the covid-19 pandemic is the only factor that will have an impact on this decision. In case we are not allowed to go in-person, we will start online.

There is not an official date yet, however we plan to start in the second half of September.

You will sign your contracts well in advance of the starting date of the Academy. We expect to complete this phase by the end of July 2021.

In order to obtain your Visa, what you need from our side is a signed contract. You should have enough time from the end of July to submit your Visa application. However, we strongly suggest to get in touch in advance with an Italian Consulate near you.

Being this a course that takes place in-person, students are required to be in Naples. This is why the equipment is usually distributed or returned at the Academy, at the beginning and at the end of the academic year.

Unfortunately the answer is no. The equipment cannot be shipped or picked up at a collection point. These operations must happen in-person, even if the Academy will start online.

Official and administrative information will always be communicated via email or the official website. Other specific means of communication will be set for the admitted students during the pre-academy time-frame to share information about the program of events and resources designed for them.

Of course there is a telephone number to get in touch with us. However, unless it’s an emergency, we kindly ask you to use emails so that we can assist you in the best way possible.

The University of Naples Federico II will be following the official Governmental Regulations. We cannot predict at this time whether there will be a vaccination policy or not.

The maximum foreseen financial contribution for participation in the training course is € 6240,84, (Six thousand two hundred forty/84 Euro) broken down as follows: 1. Attendance allowance 2. Awards The attendance allowance is equal to a maximum total amount of € 4.602,65  (Four thousand six hundred and two/65 Euro), subject to tax withholding as this is equivalent to employment income. It can only be provided for students who are unemployed and not receiving any payment in lieu of remuneration or who are seeking their first employment. The Awards are linked to the completion of Challenges related to the creation of software applications and/or start-ups for a maximum total amount of € 1638,19 (One thousand six hundred and thirty-eight/85 Euro) gross of withholding taxes as provided for by law. These can only be applicable for students who are unemployed and not in receipt of any payment in lieu of salary or who are seeking their first job.

Sadly we cannot change the tax rules for the provision of the scholarship, therefore we are unable to make any exception. Before going to Naples make sure you have enough funds to cover the costs of your stay during the Academy period.

The local transport company of Naples (ANM) provides special discounts for students. You can check this link for more information: http://www.anm.it/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=1950&Itemid=493

There are plenty of options. After signing the contract you will be given access to our internal communication channels that will help you finding an accommodation and will also provide additional information and resources about important aspects of daily life in Naples.

The Academy will be offering a program of events and a set of curated resources. The goal is to allow those who want to start exploring on their own to get inspired and find a place where to start from.
